# Quantum Entanglement: Spooky Action at a Distance


## What is Quantum Entanglement?


Quantum entanglement occurs when two or more particles become fundamentally connected, so that the quantum state of each particle cannot be described independently of the others, even when separated by vast distances.


## Key Concepts


Einstein called this phenomenon "spooky action at a distance" because when you measure one entangled particle, you instantly know the state of its partner particle, no matter how far apart they are.


## Applications


Scientists use quantum entanglement in quantum computing, quantum cryptography, and quantum teleportation. These applications could revolutionize how we process information and secure communications in the future.


## Common Examples


The most frequently cited example is the entangled pair of electrons with opposite spins - when one electron is measured as "spin up," its entangled partner will always be "spin down," instantaneously, regardless of the distance between them.


## Challenges


Maintaining quantum entanglement is extremely difficult because the particles need to be completely isolated from their environment, as any interaction can cause them to lose their entangled state through a process called decoherence.
